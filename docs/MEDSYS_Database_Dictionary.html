1. Scope and Reading Guide

This document provides a field-level dictionary for the MEDSYS PostgreSQL schema. It focuses on database-enforced validation and structure: data type, required versus nullable, default values, generated values, primary keys, unique constraints, foreign keys, indexes, and check constraints.

Important: application-level validations may be stricter than the database. This document records what the database itself enforces. Migration V8 adds client/demo seed data, V9 adds user-level extra permission storage, V10 adds patient codes plus guardian identity fields, V11 adds doctor workflow identity, and V12 adds persistent multi-role support.

2. Global Design Rules

  • Most business tables are organization-scoped through organization_id.
  • Most tables use surrogate numeric primary keys via BIGSERIAL or a sequence-backed BIGINT.
  • Many business tables use soft deletion through nullable deleted_at.
  • Appointments and audit logs are partitioned for operational scale.
  • Authentication uses refresh-token family tracking for replay detection.

12. Constraint and Validation Highlights

  • NOT NULL enforcement: identity, relationship, and workflow-critical fields are mandatory in the schema, including patients.first_name, patients.last_name, patients.full_name, patients.dob, and patients.age.
  • Unique keys: used for deduplication and natural identifiers such as user email per organization, patient NIC per organization, token id, family code, and SKU.
  • Foreign keys: enforce patient, appointment, encounter, prescription, inventory, and actor relationships.
  • Enum validation: several status and category fields are strictly database-constrained enums.
  • Check constraints: explicit length validation exists on patient_history_entries.note.
  • Generated columns: patients.full_name is database-generated and stored.
  • Partitioning: appointments and audit_logs are partitioned for scale and maintenance.
